Tuning the Orchestra

Hello, my name is Joey Hobrecker and I'm a second year Industrial Design Bachelor student at the TU/E. My studies are driven by a single idea – How can I create designs that can restore a natural balance between human nature and the digital world?

Figure 1: Me- Joey Hobrecker.

Setting the Stage

My first year was a chaotic mess; I learned the basic of coding, made messy and broken prototypes and finished the year with a typical, ugly and simple portfolio (when something is Highlighted green in this portfolio it will lead to an example in a different tab when you click on it). Studying in a choatic mess was fun, but I had to increaze the bar in the second year.

So in year 2 the sounds completely changed. Having to retake Physics and Calculus made me realize I had to put in more effort, I really had to start singing. The five expertise areas were no longer just instruments; they were the strings, brass and bass lines I needed to use to create my own harmony, my own song. I needed to stop improvising and start to orchestrate.

Personal Credo

Music is the space between the notes, so are my values for designs. I see value in the gaps between people, technology and nature. So, my credo is "Design the Intervals".
