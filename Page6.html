Creativity and Aesthetics

Description

I pushed myself creatively with several projects this year. I did UI and UX design for the Kitchencompass prototypes and my own Fina Apps, I build immersive escape room for Designing Connected Experiences, I created several aesthetic products for my work at Zeliox and I created fun characters for my Design for Games & Play II game. I also created creative alarm clocks for aesthetics of interaction. With my work in Zeliox I created several aesthetic products.

Feelings

It were exciting creative explorations but I also felt a lot of frustration. Making wild ideas into a reality, like the alarm clocks for Aesthetics of Interaction, is fun but hard to make intuitive or visually coherent. I often lacked the time or focus to finish aesthetics.

Evaluation

In this school year I learned to critically reflect on how my interactions function and feel, for example in Aesthetics of interaction and the Kitchencompass prototypes. I realized that my UI design was often confusing. I often added features because I could, not because they were needed. At my work I learned how hard it is to create an aesthetically pleasing and interesting product. I also learned how to create aesthetically pleasing and fun designs for characters.

Analysis

I now know that aesthetics is about the experience and clarity, not mindless decoration. A cluttered UI can ruin the whole product. My new insights have helped me to simplify and iterate with more purpose.

Conclusion

I matured my creative identity. I learned how to integrate aesthetic design into my complete design process. Aesthetics need testing, restraint and narrative cohesion.

Action Plan

Next year I will improve my aesthetic design by continuing to work at Zeliox.
